# Handover Intelligence Document — Template Structure

This template defines the 14-section structure that the Stage 3 LLM must produce.
Each section begins with the sentinel marker `### SECTION: [Section Name]`.
This file is a reference specification — it is not parsed at runtime.

---

### SECTION: Document Header

- Role title, department, business name
- Date of handover document generation
- Departing employee (first name or role reference only, based on sensitivity flags)
- Document prepared by: KnowledgeKeeper | Nukode
- Confidentiality notice if applicable

---

### SECTION: Risk Summary

All Risk Flags identified during Stage 2, grouped by severity:

**Critical** — requires immediate action before departure date
**High** — requires action within first 2 weeks of replacement starting
**Medium** — should be addressed within first 30 days

For each flag: state what the risk is, why it matters, and what action is recommended.

---

### SECTION: Role Overview

A narrative description of the role as it was actually performed — not the job description version:
- What the role exists to do
- How it fits into the wider team and business
- The balance between process execution, decision-making, and relationship management
- What a typical week looks like
- When and why the role spikes in demand

---

### SECTION: Knowledge Transfer — [Priority 1 Name]

Sub-section for the highest-priority knowledge category:
- What was captured
- Key people, systems, or processes involved
- Critical context the replacement needs to understand
- Known gaps or partially captured areas
- Recommended actions for the replacement

---

### SECTION: Knowledge Transfer — [Priority 2 Name]

Same structure as Priority 1 for the second-priority knowledge category.

---

### SECTION: Knowledge Transfer — [Priority 3 Name]

Same structure as Priority 1 for the third-priority knowledge category.

---

### SECTION: Key Relationships

A named contact directory with context for each relationship:
- Name, organisation, role
- Nature of the relationship
- Communication preferences or sensitivities
- Current status or outstanding items
- Recommended first action for the replacement

---

### SECTION: Systems and Access

A complete list of all systems used in the role:
- System name and purpose
- Access level held
- Transfer status (transferred / pending / unknown)
- Known quirks, workarounds, or non-obvious usage notes

---

### SECTION: In-Flight Items

All projects, negotiations, decisions, or tasks currently in progress:
- Item name and description
- Current status
- Next action required and deadline if applicable
- Who else is involved
- What the replacement needs to do first

---

### SECTION: Decision-Making Logic and Judgment

Named judgment scenarios:
- Scenario: [Name]
- Context: When does this situation arise?
- How the departing employee approached it
- Key factors they weighed
- Rule of thumb or principle
- Watch out for: common mistakes or edge cases

---

### SECTION: Undocumented Knowledge

Everything captured that has never been written down before:
- [Topic]
- What it is
- Why it matters
- How to apply it

---

### SECTION: Advice to Your Replacement

Written preserving the employee's voice and intent:
- The one thing they most want their replacement to understand
- What to do in the first two weeks
- Who to speak to early and why
- What to never do
- What success looks like in this role

---

### SECTION: Recommended Onboarding Plan

Week 1 — Orientation: specific actions based on in-flight items and key relationships
Week 2 — Relationship Building: specific contact recommendations and conversations
Week 3 — Process Immersion: which processes to shadow, learn, or take ownership of
Week 4 — Independence: what the replacement should do independently, what to escalate

---

### SECTION: Knowledge Gaps and Recommended Follow-Up

Transparent record of areas where knowledge capture was incomplete:
- What was not captured and why
- The risk level if this gap is not addressed
- Recommended way to fill the gap (ask a specific colleague, review a specific document, etc.)
